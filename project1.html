OVERVIEW.

The Canada Indie Film Festival is a celebration of the diverse and creative voices within the independent film community. The festival's design concept revolves around the idea of "hidden gems," symbolizing the unique and often overlooked treasures that indie films represent. Unlike mainstream movies, which cater to broad audiences and follow predictable formulas, indie films thrive on originality, bold storytelling, and artistic freedom. These films are like rare gems, each with its own distinct sparkle, waiting to be discovered by those who seek something different and meaningful. The festival aims to bring these hidden gems to light, offering audiences a fresh perspective on cinema that challenges the norms of the mainstream industry.

DESIGN
PROCESS.

The design process for the Canada Indie Film Festival is thoughtfully crafted to enhance the visitor experience and establish a strong event identity. The festival spans three major stops—Vancouver, Toronto, and Alberta—each represented by a distinct color: green for Vancouver, blue for Toronto, and yellow for Alberta. These colors serve as visual cues, making it easy for visitors to navigate the event and engage with the unique character of each stop. By assigning specific colors to each location, the design not only helps differentiate the festival's identity across different regions but also creates a cohesive and immersive experience that resonates with the festival's theme of uncovering hidden gems in the world of indie film.
